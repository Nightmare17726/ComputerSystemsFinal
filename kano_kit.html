Shane Irons on Kano Kits

In class, the Kano kits were reluctant to work a lot of the time, but the times my group got them to function, it was a cool experience with basic coding. In the kits, we set up a LAN connection chat room, which did not want to work, hacked Minecraft, created and moved around files using the command prompt, and some other activities utilizing the Kano coding. Looking past some of the faults found in the SD cards, OS, and keyboards, the Kano kits, when working properly, were a fun and innovative way for me to learn coding and system mechanics.



Jordan Mason on Kano Kits

Our time using the Kano Kits was brief, but fixing them and figuring out how they're supposed to work was helpful in learning how networks actually functioned. It was different from trying to work with our own computers, but the various features to explore were interesting and informative. We struggled at first, but eventually figured out what we were meant to do, and learned some things about coding and networking.
