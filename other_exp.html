Chase Franse's Other Experiences

I think that MindTap was equally a waste of time, and a good resource. I understand that most of my colleagues have choosen a side on this, but I still sit somewhere in the middle. The biggest issue, in my opinion, with the MindTap lessons are the SAM Training sections. Shane Irons's Other Experiences

MindTap was were most grades derived from. Most assignments, tests, and readings were through MindTap. The worst part of this was the "training" which consisted of 20-50 videos, that were non-skippable, and had questions during or after each. This took up a lot of time and often were questions that were self-explained. The tests and quizzes covered the material found in the reading and were not too easy nor too challenging. It was a good balance that matched the pace of the class, and I never felt swamped with too much work.



Jordan Mason's Other Experiences

Much of our at-home work was done on MindTap, where we were refreshed on basic computer concepts and taught how certain things work. The majority of our assignments were questions interlaced through a series of Youtube videos about the concept of the chapter. These were mostly informational and useful, but the questions were usually quite easy. The quizzes and tests that followed, however, were more difficult and also good learning tools. The assignments were given at a pretty forgiving pace.
